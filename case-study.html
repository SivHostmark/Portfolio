01 — Problem

What was the challenge?

Store associates were spending an increasing share of their day on manual data-labeling tasks — a necessary input for the company's machine learning pipeline, but a poor use of human time and expertise. Accuracy was inconsistent, throughput was too low, and employee satisfaction was suffering.

The challenge: design a human-in-the-loop workflow that made data labeling fast, intuitive, and accurate — without burdening the people doing it.

The Friction

Associates switched between multiple tools and had no clear feedback on quality or progress. Tasks felt arbitrary, disconnected from outcomes they could understand.

The Stakes

ML model performance depended directly on label quality. Poor data meant poor predictions — which had direct downstream impact on store operations and revenue.

02 — Research & Approach

Understanding the people
and the process

I embedded with store teams across three locations to observe real labeling sessions, conducted 14 contextual interviews, and mapped the end-to-end workflow with service designers and ML engineers together.

Key insights

Associates wanted to feel competent, not just compliant. Clear task descriptions, immediate confirmation of correct answers, and visible progress reduced anxiety and increased throughput by up to 40% in early prototype tests.

03 — Design Process

From concepts
to tested iterations

I ran four design sprints over eight weeks, moving from rough wireframes through mid-fi prototypes tested with real associates, to a refined high-fidelity design handed off to engineering. Each iteration was driven by quantitative task-completion data and qualitative feedback from Dovetail.

Wireframes — v1
Mid-fi Prototype
Hi-fi Design

Progression from lo-fi sketches through mid-fi testing to final high-fidelity screens.

Critical design decision

We moved from a list-based task queue to a card-by-card focus mode. This reduced cognitive load significantly — one task at a time, one decision at a time.

What we cut

An early "gamification" concept with points and leaderboards was dropped after testing. Associates found it condescending. Quiet, meaningful feedback worked better.

04 — Solution

A focused, human-first
labeling experience

The final design is a streamlined mobile-first web app that presents one task at a time, gives immediate AI-assisted guidance when confidence is low, and closes the feedback loop so associates can see how their work contributes to model quality.

The interface deliberately disappears — minimal chrome, high-contrast decisions, keyboard-accessible from day one.

Final Solution — Full Interface

Final high-fidelity design — data labeling interface, mobile web, 2024.

AI-assisted confidence

When the model is unsure, it surfaces a subtle hint to the associate — not the answer, but a nudge. This keeps humans in control while reducing labeling errors by 28%.

Progress that means something

A real-time "contribution meter" shows how labeled data feeds into model retraining — connecting daily work to a larger outcome that associates can feel proud of.
